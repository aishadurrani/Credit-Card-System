Credit Card System

Case Study - Data Engineering






Introduction

Credit Card System is a prototype, built to mirror a Bank Card payment system used to settle financial transactions through the transfer of monetary value. This includes the institutions, instruments, people, rules, procedures, standards, and technologies that make this exchange possible.   Payment systems are used in lieu of tendering cash in domestic and international transactions. This can be a major service provided by banks and other financial institutions.


A card is issued to a users as a method of payment for every day/month expenses. It allows the cardholder to pay for goods and services based on the holders promise to pay for them. There are a number of payment cards, the most common being credit cards and debit cards. A payment card is electronically linked to an account or accounts belonging to the cardholder.




Credit Card System (CCS) application:


This Credit Card System Application will include fields that can be adjusted and viewed by the managers of the Bank.


On the surface, the credit card transaction process seems simple: Customers swipe their cards, and before they know it, the transaction is complete. Behind every swipe, however, is a profoundly more complex procedure than what meets the eye. In fact, sliding the card and signing the receipt are only the first and final steps of a complicated procedure. And the whole process — though it happens in merely a few seconds — involves multiple stages and several actors working behind the scenes.



This Bank Credit Card Management System can create, delete or modify customer and account details by a bank admin. This system is built not only to extract stored data but in fact update any changes made to it.



    Below are some of the most important applications used in the process of creating and maintaining this system:


  • MySQL ( RDBMS )

  • JDBC (Java API)

  • Data Extraction and Transformation with Sqoop

  • Data Loading with Hive

  • Automation & Optimization with Oozie

  • Data Visualization in Hive

    • System Perspective

    The Credit Card System is an independent software system developed to manage activities like registering new customer, approving or cancelling their requests, etc. using this architecture.



    Impact of the System

    This is a new product which is developed for internal users. Expected impact of the product is to automate existing manual processes in order to make them more efficient and cost effective.
